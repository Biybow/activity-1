Favorite Places/Target Places

  1. Boracay

    BORACAY is a small island in the central Philippines. It's known for its resorts and beaches.

  2. Amanpulo

    Blissfully placed in the Cuyo Archipelago of northern Palawan in the Philippines, Amanpulo lies on the secluded Pamalican Island, fringed with white sand beaches and pristine reefs. Offering a back to nature experience in supreme comfort and privacy, this tropical paradise is lapped by calm clear seas ideal for water sports, while sandy tracks lead to deserted coves and jungle-clad lookouts.

  3. Palawan

    Palawan, officially the Province of Palawan, is an archipelagic province of the Philippines that is located in the region of Mimaropa. It is the largest province in the country in terms of total area of 14,649.73 km².

  4. Calatagan, Batangas

    Calatagan, officially the Municipality of Calatagan, is a municipality in the province of Batangas, Philippines. According to the 2020 census, it has a population of 58,719 people. Calatagan comprises the Calatagan Peninsula between the West Philippine Sea and Balayan Bay.

  5. Ilocos

    Ilocos is a region in the Philippines, encompassing the northwestern coast of Luzon island. It’s known for its historic sites, beaches and the well-preserved Spanish colonial city of Vigan. Dating from the 16th century, Vigan’s Mestizo district is characterized by cobblestone streets and mansions with wrought-iron balconies. Farther north, Laoag City is a jumping-off point for the huge La Paz Sand Dunes.

  6. Kyoto, Japan

    Kyoto, Japan Ancient temples, peaceful bamboo groves, and traditional tea ceremonies await in this city where Japan's spiritual and cultural heart beats strongest. Walking through the torii gates of Fushimi Inari shrine at sunset is an experience that transcends time.

  7. Santorini, Greece

    Santorini, Greece buildings cascade down volcanic cliffs like frozen waves, while the deep blue Aegean Sea stretches to the horizon. Watching the sunset from Oia creates memories that linger long after the golden hour fades.

  8. Iceland

    Iceland Where else can you witness a geyser eruption, walk between tectonic plates, bathe in geothermal springs, and chase the Northern Lights—all in a single day? Iceland's otherworldly landscapes feel like visiting another planet.

  9. Patagonia, Argentina/Chile

    Patagonia, Argentina/Chile At the edge of the world, jagged mountain peaks pierce clouds while massive glaciers carve their ancient paths. Here, wilderness stretches beyond imagination, offering the purest connection to Earth's raw, untamed beauty.

  10. New Zealand

    New Zealand From misty fjords to rolling green hills, this island nation packs every landscape imaginable into its compact borders. Whether seeking adrenaline in Queenstown or tranquility in Milford Sound, New Zealand's natural diversity is unmatched.
